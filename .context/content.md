Welcome to St. Stephen’s
Homeless JesusWe are an inclusive and affirming Anglican community in the heart of the city, where we strive to live out God’s mission of compassion and justice for all people, and for all of creation.

We are committed to being a community of solidarity with those who have been pushed to the margins of our society, and to the task of building a better world.

We support and engage with the arts—music, literature, theatre, the visual arts—and welcome collaboration with working artists.

# Worship

Our principal in-person worship service is a Sung Mass on Sunday at 10:30 am. This service includes music, incense, and a full homily. We use the Book of Alternative Services. Many members of the congregation are involved as readers, servers, intercessors, and singers.

Our principal on-line service is on Zoom, on Saturdays at 7 pm. This is usually ante-communion with hymns and a homily, but sometimes a full Mass. There is also a time of informal conversation afterwards. Contact us for Zoom log-in details.

Said Mass takes place on Wednesday at noon, in the Lady Chapel of the church.

Evening prayer (from the Book of Common Prayer) takes place on Monday, Tuesday and Wednesday at 4:30 pm (please note the new time).
Any lay person can lead evening prayer: contact Mtr. Maggie if you are interested.

# Children

We believe that children learn best by being part of a multigenerational faith community, and we are always happy to have children in church during our services. We understand that little ones need to move around and explore their environment, and sometimes they need to make noise. Parents should never worry that their children are “disrupting” worship; the noise and activity of children is part of the wholeness of community.

All children, including infants, are welcome to receive communion as soon as they are baptized.

# Outreach

Community Health Team

We understand health to include all aspects of life, including the social determinants of health, and relationships of support. When we speak of our �community health team�, we use this term to express that we aim at improving the health of the community as a whole, not only individuals.

Accordingly, we build long-term relationships through our drop-in, and through our connections with encamped people in the community, especially in the yard outside the church. Based on this, we work with people to identify their goals, and to assist them in meeting those goals. Our supports include accompaniment at medical, legal, and social service appointments; basic daily health supports; open-ended pastoral conversation; assistance in dealing with bureaucratic and other barriers to care and shelter; and, perhaps most of all, stable social connections without judgement or stigma. This is community-building work which may extend over months or years. We do not provide formal case management, but we do work closely with case managers as much as possible.

# Weekend Breakfast Program

Every Saturday and Sunday morning for more than thirty years, St Stephen’s has served a free breakfast for low-income and food-insecure members of the community. Currently, we provide between 200 and 250 breakfasts each Saturday and each Sunday. The programme is staffed by volunteers and funded largely by donations from supporters. # Safe Space Drop In

Our Safe Space drop-in is open every Friday night, from 6 pm until 10 pm. We offer a hot meal, a welcoming space, harms reduction supplies, and volunteers who can provide conversation, social support, frontline crisis interventions, and referrals to appropriate services.

Our drop-in is a place where we build relationships and share life experiences. For the volunteers, it is an opportunity to come to a deeper understanding of the challenges faced by some of the most marginalized people in our community. For everyone, we strive to make it a place of safety, trust, and health.

Thanks to our funders, Faithworks, the Emerald Foundation, and the Robert Kerr Foundation. Thank you also to the Daily Bread Food Bank.

# Our Neighbourhood

Kensington Market is a remarkable neighbourhood, diverse, creative, and inclusive, with a long history of connections to radical politics and art practices. It has long been the neighbourhood where new immigrant communities settle and build lives. But the community is also under threat from inappropriate development, and short-term rentals which are depriving the area of housing.

There are many social housing developments in the Market, whose residents are often food-insecure and depend on services from the church and community agencies. There are also an increasingly large number of community members who are homeless, and who live on the streets and in alleyways, or in encampments in Bellevue Square Park, Sonja’s Park, Doctor’s Parkette, and the yard of St Stephen’s itself. Many of these people have extreme and complex health needs, partly due to the trauma of long-term homelessness.

We have long-standing relationships with Friends of Kensington Market, a grassroots neighbourhood organization working to protect and promote the character of Kensington, and with the Kensington Market Community Land Trust, of which Mother Maggie was a founding member. We also work closely with The Neighbourhood Group (formerly St Stephen’s Community House, founded by an earlier rector, the Reverend Cam Russell).

# Poverty and Income Inequality

Evening prayer during a sit-in at Metro Hall, asking for an emergency cold shelter
Evening prayer during a sit-in at Metro Hall, asking for an emergency cold shelter

While we continue to try to meet the immediate needs of our community, we also know that band-aid solutions are not enough, and that we must work together to create a world in which no one has to line up in a church for nutritious food or winter shelter. We maintain close connections with the poverty reduction committee of the Diocese and the advocacy committee of St Stephen’s Community House, and we are engaged in lobbying all levels of government for policies which will reduce poverty, including raising the minimum wage and building more affordable housing.
